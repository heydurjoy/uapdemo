Grading in UAP standards

Demo policy snapshot:
  • Letter grades (e.g. A+, A, …) follow the published scale; numerical cut-offs are fixed per program.
  • Continuous assessment and terminal exam weights match the approved syllabus—no ad-hoc changes mid-term.
  • Moderation: cohort-wide drift triggers review; individual appeals follow the registrar’s calendar.
  • Marks are entered through approved channels; provisional vs. final states are clearly labeled.

Basic must know rules

Expand each chapter once. Attestation unlocks after all sections are opened.

0 / 5 sections opened Attestation locked until all open
1. Centralized exams & question papers ▸

All end-of-term and makeup exams run on the centralized calendar. Question papers must be submitted by the deadline in the approved template; late submissions require written approval from the chair.

  • Version control: v1.0 to the exam cell, amendments only through moderation.
  • No exam may be taken out of the secure print chain without authorization.
2. Moderation, mapping & blueprint ▸

Every paper must map to the approved course outline and OBE assessment plan. Moderation meetings are minuted; dissenting views are recorded.

  • Blueprint coverage: CLOs and Bloom levels declared before printing.
  • Parallel papers (A/B sets) must be statistically balanced where policy requires.
3. Invigilation, identity & seating ▸

Invigilators must arrive 20 minutes early, verify ID cards, enforce seating plans, and report incidents immediately to the exam controller.

  • Mobile devices: policy-compliant storage only; no exceptions without controller sign-off.
  • Student arrivals after the grace window follow the late-entry rule in the student handbook.
4. Malpractice, continuity & special needs ▸

Suspected malpractice follows a sealed process: seize materials, fill the incident form, no informal penalties. Students with accommodations must match the registrar’s letter.

  • Power or building continuity: follow the “exam in progress” escalation tree.
  • Do not discuss suspected cases in departmental corridors.
5. Scripts, retention & remarking ▸

Answer scripts are tracked by packet. Retention periods and destruction are set by policy; remarking windows and fees are published each term.

  • Faculty may not retain student originals for personal files.
  • Re-evaluation requests go through the exam office only.
